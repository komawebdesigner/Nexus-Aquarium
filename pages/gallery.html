Dolphine

Common Name : Dolphin
Scientific Name : Delphinidae
Location Found :  
Breeding : Copulation occurs belly to belly initiating gestation period. Dolphin mothres usually go to shallow waters to give birth to their calves assisted by another dolphin.
Diet : The most common dietry staple for dolphins is fish. Deep water dolphins eat squiud or jelly fish.
Physical Description : They are 7-10 feet long (2-3 feet) with a smooth rubbery skin that is either black,white or grey. They have flippers on their sides as well as a triangular fin on their back.

 

Turtle

Common Name : Turtle.
Scientific Name : Testudines.
Location Found : Green sea turtles are found in the Atlantic Ocean, Gulf of Mexico, Mediterranean Sea, and Indo-Pacific. West coasts of North and South America, from central Baja California to Peru. Black sea turtles are found along the west coasts of North and South America, from Baja California to Peru.
Breeding : They mate in cold months, court by moving tail side to side to allow cloacal openings to tough.They lay thier eggs in soil where they will hatch 55-125 days later.
Physical Description : They have a streamlined shell called a Carapace that is olive green, yellow or sometimes greenish brown in colour. They have limbs called flappers and are adapted for swimming, the hind limbs serve as rudders used to stabilize and direct them in the water.

 

Blue Ringed Octopus

Common Name : Blue-ringed octopus.
Scientific Name : Hapalochlaena lunulata.
Location Found : The greater blue-ringed octopus is widespread trough out the tropical and subtropical waters of the Indo-West Pacific from Sri Lanka to the Philippines and from Australia to south Japan.[5]
Breeding : The mating ritual for the blue-ringed octopus begins when a male approaches a female and begins to caress her with his modified arm, the hectocotylus. A male mates with a female by grabbing her, which sometimes completely obscures the female's vision, then transferring sperm packets by inserting his hectocotylus into her mantle cavity repeatedly. Mating continues until the female has had enough, and in at least one species the female has to remove the over-enthusiastic male by force. Males will attempt copulation with members of their own species regardless of sex or size, but interactions between males are most often shorter in duration and end with the mounting octopus withdrawing the hectocotylus without packet insertion or struggle.
Physical Description : They can be identified by their characteristic blue and black rings, and yellowish skin. When the octopus is agitated, the brown patches darken dramatically, iridescent blue rings, or clumps of rings, appear and pulsate within the maculae. Seal

Common Name : Seal.
Scientific Name : Phocidae.
Location Found : Harp, ringed, hooded, spotted, bearded and ribbon seals live in the Arctic. Crabeater, Weddell, leopard and Ross seals live in the Antarctic. Fur seals and sea lions live in the Northern Pacific between Asia and North America and off the coasts of South America, Antarctica, southwestern Africa and southern Australia.
Breeding :  
Physical Description : Males are 1.4-2.0 metres weighing 70-170kiligrams while females are 1.2-1.7 metres and weigh 50-150 kilograms. They have a fusiform body that is grey to silver with dark spots. They have limbs modified into flippers, the fore limbs have claws used to scratch,groom and defence while the hind limbs are used to move them side to side and propel them in the water.

 

Shark

Common Name : Shark.
Scientific Name : Selachimorpha.
Location Found : Sharks live in all oceans, down to depths of 2,000 meters (6,600 feet).
Breeding : Male sharks have two external reproductive structures, called claspers, which they use to transfer sperm to females. It seems cruel, but remember that white sharks need to keep moving all the time. It would be difficult for both of them to stop to mate, so one of them has to hang on to the other. Luckily, females have tougher skin than males to accommodate this. To stay alive shark embryos eat the unfererlized eggs in their mothers womb before they are born,this practice is called oophagy.
Physical Description : They have an elongate fusiform body with fins that are rigid and supported by cartilaginous rods.
